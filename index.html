Project Gallery

Single-cell RNA Sequencing Reveals the Temporal Diversity and Dynamics of Cardiac Immunity after Myocardial Infarction （ Co-first author，Small methods. 2022, e2100752.）

Temporal regulation of cardiac immunity is strongly associated with the onset, progression, and outcomes of MI. Our study of time-series scRNA-seq on the whole cardiac immune cells isolated from the LAD ligation mice and the tanshinone IIA-treated mice shed light on the underlying pathology of MI.

The Role of Müller Cells in Human Retinal Degenerative Diseases and Their Application in Drug Screening ( Co-first author，in pipeline for submission)

The preference of many conditions, such as AMD, for the human macula, suggests it may be more susceptible to stress than the peripheral retina. Our study of scRNA-seq on macular and peripheral retina samples from four donors cultured ex vivo with or without exposure to light stress revealed the potential therapeutic targets in AMD

Multiple Analyses of Brain Regions in 5XFAD Mice Reveal the Underlying Mechanisms of Alzheimer's Disease（The main participants）

This project aims to promote the fusion of multiple innovative techniques and facilitate understanding of the pathological mechanisms of some diseases, like Alzheimer's disease. Specifically, Spatial transcriptomics (ST) and magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) techniques were utilized to reconstruct a spatial multimodal atlas of the Alzheimer's disease mouse brain.
